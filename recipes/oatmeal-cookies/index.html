Oatmeal Cookies

Wholesome, hearty, and full of comforting flavor, Oatmeal Cookies are a timeless favorite that never goes out of style. Made with old-fashioned oats, these cookies offer a perfect blend of chewiness and warmth, often complemented by hints of cinnamon, brown sugar, and vanilla. Each bite feels like a nostalgic reminder of homemade goodness and simple pleasures.


Whether you enjoy them plain, studded with raisins, or loaded with chocolate chips and nuts, Oatmeal Cookies bring together texture and taste in the most satisfying way. Perfect for breakfast treats, afternoon snacks, or sharing with loved ones, they're a cookie that feels both classic and wholesome — a little taste of comfort in every bite.



Ingredients

  • 4 ½ cups gluten-free old-fashioned oats, divided
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 ½ teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon gluten-free baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon fine salt
  • 2 sticks unsalted butter, room temperature
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • ½ cup packed light-brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • ¾ cup chocolate chips
  • ¾ cup raisins

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment, In a blender, process 1 1/2 cups oats into a fine meal.
  2. Whisk together ground oats, cornstarch, cinnamon, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, using a mixer, beat butter and sugars until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in eggs and vanilla. With mixer on low, gradually add oat mixture, beating until combined.
  5. Fold in chocolate chips, raisins, and remaining 3 cups oats.
  6. In batches, drop dough in 2-tablespoonful mounds onto prepared baking sheet, 2 inches apart.
  7. Bake until cookies are golden brown at edges, 15 to 18 minutes. Let cool on sheet on a wire rack 2 minutes, then transfer to rack and let cool completely.
