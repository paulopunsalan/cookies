Whoopie Pies

Soft, fluffy, and filled with sweet creamy goodness, Whoopie Pies are a delightful dessert that blurs the line between cake and cookie. Traditionally made with two pillowy chocolate cakes sandwiched around a smooth vanilla or marshmallow filling, these treats offer the perfect combination of rich flavor and fun, nostalgic charm.


Originating from the kitchens of the northeastern United States — particularly Pennsylvania Amish country — Whoopie Pies have long been a symbol of homemade comfort and joy. Whether you stick to the classic chocolate-and-cream pairing or experiment with flavors like pumpkin, red velvet, or peanut butter, these irresistible handheld desserts are sure to bring smiles to every occasion.



Ingredients

For the Chocolate Whoopie Pie Cookies
  • 1 ¾ cups unbleached all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ¾ cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 ½ teaspoons baking soda
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup buttermilk, room temperature
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
For the Vanilla Buttercream Filling
  • ½ cup (1 stick) unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 3 to 4 cups confectioners' sugar
  • ¼ cup milk
  • ½ teaspoon pure vanilla extract

Directions

  1. Make the cookies: Preheat oven to 400 degrees. Sift together flour, salt, cocoa powder, baking soda, and baking powder. Set aside. Line 2 baking pans with parchment paper. In the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, cream butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add eggs, buttermilk, and vanilla. Beat until well combined. Slowly add dry ingredients. Mix until combined.
  2. Using a 1-ounce ice cream scoop, place dough onto a parchment-lined baking pan, 12 per pan. Bake for 10 to 12 minutes. Remove to a rack to cool. Repeat with remaining batter.
  3. Make the vanilla buttercream: In the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, cream butter until smooth and creamy, 2 to 3 minutes. With mixer on low speed, add 3 cups sugar, milk, and vanilla; mix until light and fluffy. If necessary, gradually add remaining cup sugar to reach desired consistency.
  4. Transfer buttercream to a disposable pastry bag and snip the end. When cookies have cooled completely, pipe 2 tablespoons buttercream onto the flat side of half the cookies. Sandwich with remaining cookies, pressing down slightly so that the filling spreads to the edge of the cookies.
